Upbringing

A lot of my life has been defined by an amalgamation of cultural influence: being born in my home country of Egypt, living most of my formative life in Kuwait, spending all my school years studying in an American school, acquiring a Hungarian passport, and going to college in the UK. I’m perhaps the picture definition of a 3rd Culture Kid, and while I do sometimes feel separated from my culture of origin in that regard, I also believe this unique upbringing resulted in me being significantly more worldly, understanding, and generally empathetic.

I have always been attached to the arts, supplemented by a fascination with experimental and theoretical sciences, physics in particular. Due to the dissonant nature of those two paths, I was often left unsure of what my role would be in the future. I initially spent most of my life thinking I was going to grow up to be a musician, however, part of me also wanted to pursue computer science or even theoretical physics.

It was only after playing Chrono Trigger for the first time in my last year of high-school that something clicked. I spent that final year playing through a massive library of games, ranging in genre and era. I was absolutely enraptured by everything regarding this medium of art, it was the dream consolidation between arts and sciences that I have always dreamed of pursuing, and I am now in a position where I have dedicated my life to the games medium, and will continue to do so.

What I Love About Games

I earnestly believe games to be the premier form of artistic expression, both for the creator and audience. There is no artistic medium more malleable and capable of such a grand variety of innovation and creativity. That also extends to audiences, as there are as many audiences as there are games in existence, with everyone approaching the artform in different means.

Due to how personal the advent of interactivity is, everyone will engage with games differently, and everyone will desire different things out of their game experiences: social connection, competitiveness, narrative, skill expression, relaxation and stress-relief, a sense of achievement, self-expression, curiosity, autonomy, cultural expression, nostalgia, and so much more.

Game Designers have the profound role of creating these deeply personal and intimate experiences for our audiences, and that’s the core of what makes Games so beautiful, and why I love creating and experiencing art within this vast medium.
